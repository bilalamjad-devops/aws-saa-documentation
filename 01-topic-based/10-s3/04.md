4. Question

Category: CSAA – Design Cost-Optimized Architectures

A Solutions Architect is working for a financial company. The manager wants to have the ability to automatically transfer obsolete data from their S3 bucket to a low-cost storage system in AWS after a certain period of time.

What is the best solution that the Architect can provide to them?

- Use Amazon Timestream.
- Use Amazon SQS.
- Use an EC2 instance and a scheduled job to transfer the obsolete data from their S3 location to Amazon S3 Glacier.
- `Use Lifecycle Policies in S3 to move obsolete data to Glacier.`

# What is an S3 Lifecycle Policy?

Think of it as a **timer** on your S3 objects.

Example:

```text
Day 1
Upload file to S3 Standard

↓

After 30 days
Move to Standard-IA

↓

After 180 days
Move to Glacier

↓

After 365 days
Delete it
```

You configure the rule **once**, and AWS does everything automatically.

---

# Correct Answer

✅ **Use Lifecycle Policies in S3 to move obsolete data to Glacier.**

Why?

Because Lifecycle Policies are specifically designed to:

* Move objects between storage classes
* Archive old files
* Delete old files automatically

No coding.
No EC2.
No cron jobs.

---

# Why the others are wrong

### ❌ Amazon Timestream

Used for:

* IoT data
* Sensor data
* Metrics
* Time-series databases

Not for moving S3 files.

---

### ❌ Amazon SQS

SQS is a **queue**.

It stores messages, not files.

Nothing to do with S3 storage classes.

---

### ❌ EC2 + Scheduled Job

Yes, it would work.

But AWS already has a managed feature for this.

In AWS exams:

> **Always choose the managed service over building your own solution.**

Lifecycle Policies are cheaper, simpler, and require almost no maintenance.
