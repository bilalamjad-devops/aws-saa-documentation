3. Question

Category: CSAA – Design Resilient Architectures

A commercial bank has a forex trading application. They created an Auto Scaling group of EC2 instances that allow the bank to cope with the current traffic and achieve cost-efficiency. They want the Auto Scaling group to behave in such a way that it will follow a predefined set of parameters before it scales down the number of EC2 instances, which protects the system from unintended slowdown or unavailability.

Which of the following statements are true regarding the cooldown period? (Select TWO.)

- Its default value is 600 seconds.
- It ensures that before the Auto Scaling group scales out, the EC2 instances have an ample time to cooldown.
- It ensures that the Auto Scaling group launches or terminates additional EC2 instances without any downtime.
- `Its default value is 300 seconds.`
- `It ensures that the Auto Scaling group does not launch or terminate additional EC2 instances before the previous scaling activity takes effect.`

Excellent. This is one of those questions where AWS uses a **confusing word** ("cooldown") that many people misunderstand.

The word **cooldown** makes people think:

> "The EC2 instance needs to cool down."

❌ **Wrong.**

In AWS, **Cooldown is for the Auto Scaling Group—not for the EC2 instance.**

This distinction is critical.

---

# Step 1: What is the question asking?

The company wants:

> Protect the system from unintended slowdown or unavailability.

Why?

Imagine this situation.

---

## Time = 9:00

CPU reaches:

```text
80%
```

ASG says:

"Launch 2 new EC2 instances."

Good.

---

## Time = 9:01

Those EC2 instances are still:

* Booting
* Running user data
* Registering with the ALB
* Passing health checks

They are **not serving traffic yet**.

---

## If ASG checks CPU immediately again...

CPU is still:

```text
80%
```

ASG might think:

> "Still busy! Launch 2 more!"

---

## Time = 9:02

Still high CPU.

Launch again.

---

Now instead of:

2 extra instances...

You accidentally launch:

8 extra instances.

Waste of money.

---

# Step 2: What does Cooldown do?

Cooldown simply says:

> **"Wait. Don't make another scaling decision yet."**

That's all.

Think of it as:

```text
Scale

↓

WAIT

↓

Observe

↓

Scale again if needed
```

---

# Step 3: Real Timeline

Without Cooldown

```text
9:00

CPU High

↓

Launch 2

↓

9:01

CPU Still High

↓

Launch 2

↓

9:02

Launch 2

↓

9:03

Launch 2
```

Chaos.

---

With Cooldown

```text
9:00

CPU High

↓

Launch 2

↓

WAIT 300 sec

↓

Instances become healthy

↓

CPU decreases

↓

No unnecessary scaling
```

Perfect.

---

# Step 4: Correct Statement #1

> Default value is 300 seconds.

This is a fact.

Default Cooldown:

**300 seconds**

=

**5 minutes**

Easy memorization.

---

# Step 5: Correct Statement #2

> Auto Scaling waits before performing another scaling activity.

Exactly.

This is the entire purpose of Cooldown.

AWS waits for:

Previous scaling action

↓

Take effect

↓

Then evaluates again.

---

# Step 6: Why Option 1 is wrong

It says:

600 seconds.

Wrong.

Default:

300.

---

# Step 7: Why Option 2 is wrong

It says:

EC2 instances cool down.

This is the trap.

Instances don't cool down.

The **Auto Scaling Group waits**.

Not the EC2.

---

# Step 8: Why Option 3 is wrong

It says:

No downtime.

Cooldown has nothing to do with:

Availability

Downtime

High Availability

It only controls:

Scaling frequency.

---

# Step 9: Architect View

```text
CPU > 70%

↓

CloudWatch Alarm

↓

ASG

↓

Launch EC2

↓

Cooldown

↓

Wait

↓

Evaluate Again
```

Notice:

The wait happens **inside ASG**.

---

# Step 10: Memory Trick

Imagine you're driving.

You press the accelerator.

The car needs a moment to speed up.

If you press it again every second...

You don't know whether the first press already worked.

Cooldown is simply:

> "Wait and see the result before pressing again."

---

# Step 11: One More Thing (Very Important)

The Tutorials Dojo explanation mentions:

> Simple Scaling Policy.

This is because **Cooldown is mainly associated with Simple Scaling policies**.

Modern AWS often uses:

* Target Tracking
* Step Scaling

These rely more on **instance warmup** than the old group cooldown behavior.

For the SAA exam, however, you should remember:

* **Default Cooldown = 300 seconds**
* **Purpose = Prevent repeated scaling before the previous scaling activity has taken effect**

# 🔥 Bilal's Golden Rule #13

**Cooldown is for the Auto Scaling Group—not for EC2 instances.**

Remember this sentence:

> **"After Auto Scaling launches or terminates instances, it waits (default 300 seconds) before making another scaling decision so the previous action has time to take effect."**

If you keep that single definition in mind, you'll answer almost every cooldown question correctly.
